You run the exercises you always run. Your hands know them cold, so nothing gets harder and nothing gets cleaner. That is the plateau, and grinding the same reps will not get you off it.

Gridding does. It takes a rhythm you already own and bends it in ways that force your hands, your reading, and your brain to catch up. One exercise becomes hundreds, and every one of them is a rep you have never played before.
